Opening Remarks Framework

The discussion was designed to bring together CEOs, regulators, academics, and practitioners to tackle real issues — cutting through the hype to address where AI works, where it fails, and how to innovate without crossing regulatory lines.

Session 1

Trends in AI and Financial Services

Rapid developments across credit, fraud prevention, trading, personalization, and operational automation — each with high potential and high stakes.

Discussion Question: What's one AI application that's exceeded your expectations? OR why is 2025 such a critical year for AI adoption in banking and what risks do institutions face if they don't act decisively?

Session 2

AI in Credit Scoring & Lending

Exploring the promise and controversy in AI credit decisioning — and whether machines can truly assess the human elements of trust and character.

Discussion Question: Can AI truly replicate human instinct in lending decisions — assessing trust or character? Or is that still a myth?

Session 3

Fraud Prevention & Risk Management

Fraudsters are becoming more sophisticated — using AI themselves. Banks face the challenge of staying ahead across real-time payments and cross-border transactions.

Discussion Question: What's an emerging fraud tactic that's forcing AI models to evolve? What does staying ahead of AI-powered fraud look like in your institution?

Session 4

AI in Trading & Investment

Discussion Question: If your AI trading bot consistently outperforms human traders for 3 years, would you replace your trading desk? Or is the human element still essential during market shocks?

Session 5

AI-Driven Personalization

How far is too far when it comes to AI personalization? Enhancing customer value versus risking customer trust — including data sharing implications.

Discussion Question: Would you share customer data with AI experts to develop better defenses for the industry? Or use open-source AI models like ChatGPT, Claude, or LLaMA for analyzing financial datasets? What are the security implications?

Follow-up: Have you seen measurable ROI in product targeting, customer service, or engagement?

Session 6

Automation & Quantum Computing Impact

As AI and quantum computing evolve, banks face both opportunity and risk. Quantum could optimize portfolios — but also break modern encryption.

Discussion Question: Which applications should banks prioritize in response: security, speed, or smarter strategy?

Lightning Round Questions

What's one AI trend you think is overhyped? And one that's underappreciated?

This discussion brief was produced by Arooj as part of her role as Advisory Committee Member and Content Strategist at the AI Association in Banking & Financial Services, New York. The content was reviewed and used in executive-level roundtable discussions with global industry leaders.
