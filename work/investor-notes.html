Investor Discussion Framework
Technology Startup

📊 Strategic Business Writing ✍️ Written by Arooj 🎯 Investor-Ready Document

This structured investor discussion framework was produced for a technology startup — covering every critical area investors evaluate before committing capital. Designed for use in actual investor meetings.

1
Why Investors Want to Invest
  • Growth potential and scalability of the business
  • Strong market opportunity — large market size and unmet customer needs
  • Solving a real problem with long-term demand
  • Strong, motivated team capable of execution
  • Attractive Return on Investment opportunity
  • Clear vision and exit strategy for investors
2
What Investors Need to Know
  • Market Research: size, competition, and opportunities
  • Customer Target: acquisition roadmap and projections
  • Problems Solved: today and what can be solved in future
  • Revenue Model: how the business makes money
  • Vision and Future Plan: long-term goals and growth strategy
  • Team Strength: skills, intellectual capacity, and execution history
  • Technology Stack: tools and systems used to operate and scale
  • Financials: revenue, projections, expenses, and use of funds
3
Customer Acquisition Roadmap
100
→
First customers — proof of concept, manual outreach, referrals
1,000
→
Scaled acquisition — paid ads, partnerships, product-market fit confirmed
1 Lakh
→
Platform scale — automated systems, brand recognition, viral loops
4
Team Management Strategy
  • People not delivering: replace promptly and transparently
  • People with potential but stuck: motivate, train, and grow
  • Core intellectual property team: treat as partners with fair rewards
  • Retention: reward top performers beyond their expectations
  • Transparent processes to build deep internal trust
5
Investor Questions Preparation
How much money do you need and in which areas will it be used?
How long until you generate profit?
How will investors make money — ROI, equity growth, exit?
How will you get your first 100, 1000, 1 lakh customers?
Who are your competitors and how are you different?
What are your current financials — P&L, debts, growth rate?
What is the market size opportunity?
What is the exit strategy for investors?
6
Investor Pitch Rules
  • Never say "we don't have competition"
  • Don't give unrealistic projections
  • Don't be unclear or overly conservative
  • Never be non-transparent about current financials
  • Always show exactly how much money you need and what % equity you offer
  • Be specific about roles and responsibilities for both sides
  • Verify every claim you make with data
  • Be transparent and practical — investors value honesty over optimism
